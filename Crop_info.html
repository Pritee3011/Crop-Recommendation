Available Crop Guide

Detailed information on optimal growing conditions and uses for each crop in our database.

Cereal

Rice

The staple food crop of a large part of the world.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Hot and humid (22°C to 32°C).
  • Rainfall: High, above 100cm/year.
  • Soil: Alluvial, clayey soil that can retain water.
Cereal

Maize

Used as both food (corn) and fodder (animal feed).

Conditions:

  • Climate: Requires moderate temperature (21°C to 27°C).
  • Rainfall: 50 to 100 cm.
  • Soil: Old alluvial soil is preferred.
Pulse

ChickPea

High protein source, crucial for vegetarian diets (Garbanzo Bean).

Conditions:

  • Climate: Cool and dry climate for growth.
  • Soil: Loamy to clayey soils, well-drained.
Pulse

KidneyBeans

Popular legume, rich in minerals and fiber.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Needs moderate temperatures (18°C to 24°C).
  • Soil: Well-drained loamy soils.
Pulse

PigeonPeas

Versatile crop, tolerates drought well (Toor Dal).

Conditions:

  • Climate: Tropical/subtropical, warm, and humid.
  • Soil: Wide range, but deep, well-drained loams are best.
Pulse

MothBeans

Hardy crop, primarily grown in arid and semi-arid regions.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Requires hot and dry conditions.
  • Soil: Sandy or loamy soils, highly drought-tolerant.
Pulse

MungBean

Fast-growing pulse, often used for sprouts (Green Gram).

Conditions:

  • Climate: Warm weather (25°C to 35°C).
  • Soil: Sandy loams to heavy clays, sensitive to waterlogging.
Pulse

Blackgram

Known for its use in Dosa and Idli batter (Urad Dal).

Conditions:

  • Climate: Warm and sunny climate.
  • Soil: Loamy or clayey loams, good drainage is vital.
Pulse

Lentil

Cool-season crop, highly nutritious (Masoor Dal).

Conditions:

  • Climate: Cool and dry growing season.
  • Soil: Sandy loam to silty clay loam, pH 6.0-8.0.
Fruit

Pomegranate

Drought-tolerant fruit known for its medicinal properties.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Semi-arid conditions, hot/dry summers.
  • Soil: Wide adaptability, but deep, well-drained loamy soils are best.
Fruit

Banana

Tropical fruit, requires high humidity and warmth.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Tropical/subtropical, 20°C to 30°C.
  • Soil: Rich, well-drained loamy soil, susceptible to waterlogging.
Fruit

Mango

The "King of Fruits," grown widely in tropical areas.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Tropical, needs high temperature and dry period for flowering.
  • Soil: Deep, well-drained loamy soils are ideal.
Fruit

Grapes

Used for direct consumption, wine, and raisins.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Requires hot/dry summer and cold winter (temperate).
  • Soil: Deep, friable, well-drained loamy soil.
Fruit

Watermelon

Summer fruit requiring intense heat for sugar development.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Hot, dry weather, 25°C to 35°C.
  • Soil: Sandy to loamy, rich in organic matter.
Fruit

Muskmelon

Warm-season crop, known for its sweet, musky flavor.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Hot and sunny, sensitive to frost.
  • Soil: Sandy loams, good drainage is necessary.
Fruit

Apple

Temperate fruit requiring a distinct chilling period.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Temperate zone, needs cold winters (chilling hours).
  • Soil: Deep, well-drained loam, pH 5.5-6.5.
Fruit

Orange

Citrus fruit that thrives in subtropical conditions.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Subtropical, warm and humid or cool and dry.
  • Soil: Wide variety, but deep, well-drained sandy or clay loams.
Fruit

Papaya

Fast-growing fruit tree, sensitive to cold and frost.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Warm, frost-free climate, 20°C to 30°C.
  • Soil: Light, well-drained sandy loam.
Nut/Oil

Coconut

Coastal tree, used for water, oil, and fiber (coir).

Conditions:

  • Climate: Tropical, humid, close to the sea is optimal.
  • Soil: Sandy, laterite, or alluvial, needs good drainage.
Fiber

Cotton

Source of natural fiber, needs intense sun and black soil.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Tropical/Subtropical, 21°C to 30°C, 210 frost-free days.
  • Soil: Black soil (regur) or deep alluvial soil.
Fiber

Jute

The "Golden Fibre," used for sacks, mats, and carpets.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Hot and humid, high rainfall (120-150 cm).
  • Soil: Well-drained alluvial soil.
Beverage

Coffee

Grown on hill slopes, highly sensitive to frost.

Conditions:

  • Climate: Tropical, high altitude, 15°C to 28°C.
  • Soil: Well-drained, deep, friable loams; often grown on hill slopes.
